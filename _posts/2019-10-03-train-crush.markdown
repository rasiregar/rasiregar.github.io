*Photo sponsored by Tiket.com (just joking but if you guys want to send me some money I wouldn’t mind)*I had no class yesterday, so I decided to spend my spare time doing college assignments in a cafe near my home. At 5 pm, I had to attend a meeting in Menteng, so I decided to go there by train since the cafe is within walking distance from the nearest train station.

Since the 4th year at college, I rarely go to other places by train. Now my classes are at Salemba, I go to college by Go-Jek. No more half-hour commute to Depok in the morning and more half-hour commute in the afternoon back to Jakarta.

So here I was, at Tebet station, checking if my e-Money still has some money left. The trip to Menteng from Tebet is rather short, with only a couple of minutes passing one station (Manggarai) and onto Cikini station. From there, I would get off the train and walk a little to the meeting place.

As I walked through the platform, I saw something that was rather unfamiliar, but fascinating. A woman, waiting. Oh, and she happened to be absolutely stunning. My feet trembled, my focus scattered, my eyes involuntarily hooked on her.

That is, my friend, what it feels like to meet a train crush.


Train crush, like the term describes itself, is the person you meet while you are on the train that is so physically appealing to you that you lose every sense of what you are doing at that moment. Like a crush in general, it crushes your feeling like a train going at full speed (except we are in it) and makes your heart warm somehow, and you don’t mind.

C’mon, fellow commuters, you understand me. You guys must have encountered your train crush at least once. Maybe if you are a rising executive at a reputable professional service company based in Sudirman, the correct term would be MRT-crush. If your office is located in a suburb or within small streets, it would be an angkot crush you are meeting. Transjakarta crush, bemo crush, oplet crush; anything. Grabcar or GoCar crush is possible, albeit much less probable since the only person you are meeting is the driver. GoJek crush would be the with the smallest probability except if you have some kind of helmet wearing fetish.

Don’t get me wrong, train crush is not only an attractive person taking public transportation that happens to meet us. We can find them more frequently. This is a whole different case. It is an attractive POS (person of the opposite sex) that is*your *type, taking public transportation. So attractive to you that you deny all the logical explanations possible regarding the person.

I don’t know what kind of primitive cognitive pathway wired into our brain for enabling us to experience meeting a crush. You meet a girl on the train, oh what a big deal. The girl happens to be attractive, again: what a big deal. Duh, you can find tons of attractive women on the Internet, online dating apps, or better: Faculty of Psychology. However, certain people with attractive physical features can make you brought down on your knees, thinking that she’s ‘the one’. What the hell are you on again, basal ganglia? Says my neocortex.

Train crush is glorious at the same time it is tragic. It is because while you are fortunately encountering a person that could warm your heart almost instantly, you can’t help but let them go back to their personal life when they got off the train. It is just like that corny tumblr post about the lines depicting relationships between people.

The closest representation of encountering a train crush would be the one night stand, though I doubt the lines would actually meet if the intersection between 2 lines would be defined as a few hours of intimate sexual intercourse.

I just hope with my dearest heart that yesterday’s case would be the “someone you meet at the wrong time, then re-meet at the right time”. Goddammit, tumblr posts, why you gotta be so darn true? I hate conceding to you, heartbroken vsco girls.

### So what happened next?

The girl stood tall, focusing on her phones, while I still can’t believe my eyes seeing an unbelievably pulchritudinous creature. The train came shortly after, we both got in.

On the train that is not too crowded but no vacant seats, we both stood opposite to each other. She looks so beautiful with the long hair and glasses being put on the head like a headband. She wore a formal attire that is a little too revealing to wear in public transportation, with open shoulders and a part of her back exposed.

Don’t get me wrong, I wholly agree that women should be wearing anything they want to wear without the patriarchs dictating them. Ideally, it should be this way. However, you can’t change the culture of Indonesians overnight. These men here are perverts. I kept looking at her surroundings, catching if any men put a constant stare to this lady right here. Fortunately, there is none that I am aware of.

I myself also couldn’t look at her constantly, it would be considered as sexual harassment. So, I just enjoyed her presence. Thinking that God brought us together in the same place gave me an irrational injection of optimism towards life.

You might think inside my brain I would think salacious thoughts. Well, you are mistaken. It’s a whole lot pettier in here.

I imagined if she is really the one, and I had the guts to actually introduce myself to her and we became acquaintances. She would work at a financial firm. We would continue on a couple of dates. And when the time is right I would propose to her, and then we would get married, made a harmonious family, and live happily ever after.

It’s funny when we are having a crush, suddenly we become overly optimistic. I don’t actually know anything about the girl, but I seem just to use the benefit of the doubt. I don’t know if the girl would turn up to be an absolute bitch, or materialistic, or even worse, not a female. But in my minds, I play our best ever scenario, that she is a brilliant executive, international relations graduate with a heart of an angel who often volunteers for humanitarian causes in South Sudan, able to speak 4 languages (with French being one of them), plays classical piano since 5 years old, and have a good taste of perfume.

But after all, it is all in my head. I decided to distract myself by reading my ebook, or at least pretending to read since I can't focus on doing anything if the woman still in the same car with me. Because I had to switch train in Manggarai, I came off the train while surprisingly, she stayed. I didn’t have the guts to say hello to her because I thought it would be hella awkward. Moreover, she’s probably older than me. She looks like she works while I am still struggling to graduate and afterward become the absolute end of the food chain in the hospital.

And our path divides once again for God-knows how long.

Long after that, I still think about this particular experience. I wish if I encounter a similar experience in the future, I would have the courage to leave a longlasting impression on them. Maybe I should bring my guitar every time I take a train so in case another train crush came up, I would be showcasing my guitar playing abilities accompanied by my subpar vocals singing John Mayer songs. It would be some precious minutes before the train guards kicked me off the train for busking. But, it would be worth it.

So dear anonymous lady, I hope you are doing well in your career, whatever that is maybe: finance, marketing, modeling, government intelligence- anything. May destiny meets us in the future, that is if God allows.

And dear me, why the hell I wrote all this when I still haven’t done my thesis!?
