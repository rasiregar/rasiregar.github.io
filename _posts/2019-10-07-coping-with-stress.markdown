## Coping with Stress

These last couple of days has been really stressful for me. The deadline for my thesis is looming closer, and my manuscript is far from completion. I don’t know what about me with stresses, but when I have a lot to think about, my thoughts disable me to think clearly.

This college life surely taught me one thing: that I got to know myself better over time, and one of the key traits that I really hate to concede is that I prone to stress.

Such a key drawback I am having, people who believed me to become the President of the Student Union should’ve known better — I should’ve known better. Good thing now that the incumbent President has a mental toughness comparable to steel.

What I am feeling right now is similar to what I felt when I am busy preparing my campaign material. Those hectic and stressful days, suddenly I had a trip down to my memory lane and my breathing stopped for a second.

Feeling totally helpless, with nothing positive comes to mind, let alone brilliant ideas to be put in the word processor. Like a writer’s block, my negative thoughts resurface and blocks all my creativity and insights.

Now that I feel like this, the first step to fix myself is to calm myself, believing that what I am feeling is totally normal. Everyone has it once in a while, feeling absolutely strangled by our own thoughts and leaving us paralyzed. To validate our feelings as normal and humane is a key step towards coping, I suppose.

Now that I have validated my feelings, what comes next? It’s with a great wonder how come I happen to not knowing my most effective coping mechanism yet. Heck, I am going to graduate in a few months (amen to that), and I still don’t know what to do to get myself better fast.

I used to think that listening to music is my most effective coping technique, but over time I have proven this to be ineffective. I tried to listen repeatedly to the upbeat hit K-Pop song so I can send a signal to my brain the confidence that the song delivers, hoping a serotonin surge. Unfortunately, my mind was too cloudy to actually visualize the song and my confidence is still in a critical level.

I like reading, I have developed a habit of reading a book an hour a day, but these last couple of days I decided to put off this habit until my thesis is finished because reading takes my cognitive space, the precious space that can be filled or used to complete my academic writing.

Now I used my last-ditch attempt to actually alleviate my turmoil of thoughts. I write. I try to crystallize things that worry me and make some sense of it. Rationalization. I don’t give a damn if my writing sucks content-wise or if my writing has many grammar mistakes, I trust Grammarly to fix it. What is important right now is me calming myself, letting go of the negative thoughts that cloud my judgment, and carry on.

Have I felt better? Well, yes but not much. I gotta be patient with myself, I suppose. The world is not going to end tomorrow with me havent completed my thesis. But if it does, why should I worry about my thesis? The world is going to end anyway.

Here’s what I gonna do. I’m going to close my laptop, have some delightful sleep, wake up fresh tomorrow, exercise, and ready to wrestle the world once again.
